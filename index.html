Dr Sima Samar

Dr. Sima SamarSima Samar is a prominent Hazara human rights advocate, activist, and medical doctor from Afghanistan. Born on February 3, 1957, she has served as the Minister of Women’s Affairs of Afghanistan from December 2001 to 2003. Dr. Samar is also known for her work with the Shuhada Organization, which operated more than 100 schools in 2012, and has been a Nobel Peace Prize nominee for her dedication to public service, humanitarian work, and women’s empowerment.
